Cost Model

How the math changes with traffic

turbopuffer charges per unit of work: $0.33/GB/month storage, $2/GB writes, and $0.001/TB scanned per query — with a 1.28 GB minimum scan per query regardless of how small your index is.


That minimum scan floor is what makes it economical for sporadic traffic and expensive for sustained load. Below ~10 QPS sustained, you may pay less than Qdrant's flat rate. Above that threshold, Qdrant's predictable monthly price wins — there's no per-query surprise on the invoice.


Qdrant Cloud starts at $26.10/month (1 node, 1 vCPU, 4 GB RAM) with fixed, predictable billing regardless of query volume. The cost curve never surprises you at end of month.

Critical caveat

Filtered search requires pinning — and pinning costs 95× more

The ~10 QPS cost crossover only applies to serverless mode. Filtered search on turbopuffer requires pinning (namespaces stay resident, no scale-to-zero). Pinned billing uses a 64 GB minimum billing floor regardless of actual data size, at $0.01325/GB-hr per replica.

For the H&M benchmark dataset (105K vectors, 2048-dim), tpuf pinned 4-replica is 95× more expensive than Qdrant while delivering 14× worse throughput and lower recall. Break-even requires exceeding the 64 GB floor, which means ~900K–1.8M vectors depending on dimensionality. Below that scale, Qdrant is cheaper at every QPS level.

Workload Analysis

Picking the right tool

turbopuffer wins when…
  • Idle multi-tenant SaaS where most tenants query infrequently
  • Dev, staging, or internal tooling with unpredictable usage
  • Async or batch search pipelines — latency is not the constraint
  • Cold-start cost matters more than cold-start latency
  • Ingestion speed is critical and query SLAs are loose
Qdrant wins when…
  • Sustained traffic above 10 QPS — cost advantage compounds quickly
  • Filtered or faceted search in production — 40× lower p99
  • User-facing APIs where a 1.5-second cold-start breaks the experience
  • Recall precision matters — 95.9% vs 90.9% on filtered H&M
  • Predictable infrastructure costs for budgeting and procurement
Bottom Line

The call

turbopuffer is a cost-efficient storage tier for sporadic, unfiltered workloads — it earns its place in dev environments and idle multi-tenant SaaS where queries arrive rarely. Qdrant is the choice for filtered search, production APIs, and any sustained traffic above 10 QPS. For filtered search specifically: pinning is required, and the 64 GB billing floor makes tpuf 95× more expensive than Qdrant on sub-5 GB datasets with no performance benefit. The cost crossover does not apply to filtered workloads.

Benchmark: DBpedia 100K (unfiltered), H&M (filtered), multi-tenant isolation. Both systems deployed in the same region. Recall measured at identical HNSW ef parameters.
